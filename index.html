Equipment Categories

Class 1 Equipment

Definition: Electrical equipment with an earth wire (e.g., fridges, kettles, toasters).

Examples: PCs, printers, monitors, photocopiers, refrigerators, kettles.

Inspection & Testing: Both visual inspection and combined inspection and testing are required depending on use.

Class 2 Equipment

Definition: Double-insulated equipment that doesn’t rely on an earth connection (e.g., drills, hairdryers).

Examples: Most handheld power tools, some household appliances like vacuum cleaners.

Inspection & Testing: Class 2 equipment typically requires less frequent testing but regular visual inspections.

Handheld Equipment

Definition: Equipment held in the hand during use (e.g., electric drills, hairdryers).

Testing Frequency: Handheld equipment often requires more frequent testing (typically every 12 months or sooner in high-risk environments).

Moveable Equipment

Definition: Equipment that can be moved while connected to a supply (e.g., extension leads, portable heaters).

Testing Frequency: Regular visual inspections (every 6 months), formal testing every 12-24 months.

Key UK Legislation Impacting PAT Testing

Electricity at Work Regulations 1989

Requires electrical systems (including appliances) to be maintained in a safe condition.

Health and Safety at Work Act 1974

Mandates employers to provide safe working conditions, including maintaining electrical safety.

Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999

Requires employers to assess risks, including those from electrical equipment.

Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998

Electrical equipment used at work must be suitable, well-maintained, and inspected to prevent hazards.
